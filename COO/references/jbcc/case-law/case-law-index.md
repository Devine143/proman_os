# JBCC Case Law Index

## Overview

This index tracks legal precedents, court judgments, and adjudication awards related to JBCC contracts. Each case is categorized by topic for quick reference when Principal Agent issues arise.

### Extensions of Time (EOT)
*Cases interpreting Clause 6.13 - Extension of Time procedures*

| Case Name | Year | Court/Forum | Key Issue | Outcome | File |
|-----------|------|-------------|-----------|---------|------|
| Pro-Khaya v City of Cape Town | 2025 | HC Western Cape | EOT claims & adjudication enforcement | City bound by adjudication determination on EOT | `Pro-Khaya_v_City_of_Cape_Town_2025.md` |
| Radon v NV Properties | Pre-2010 | HC/SCA | Time bars on EOT claims; revised claims | Initial claims time-barred; revised claims with new info valid | `Radon_v_NV_Properties.md` |

**Key Principles:**
- Principal Agent must act fairly and impartially when assessing EOT claims - even if favors contractor
- PA EOT determinations subject to 20-day dispute notice deadline; if not challenged, becomes final
- Contractors can revise EOT claims post-completion based on new information (not mere re-argument)
- PA must consider revised claims based on genuinely new information
- Strict procedural compliance essential - time bars rigorously enforced

### Variations (Clause 6.19)
*Cases on variation orders, scope changes, and Principal Agent instructions*

| Case Name | Year | Court/Forum | Key Issue | Outcome | File |
|-----------|------|-------------|-----------|---------|------|
| LBMC v Minister of Public Works | 2023 | High Court | Defective BOQ; undisclosed asbestos; wrongful termination | Contractor entitled to variations for BOQ errors and asbestos; termination premature | `LBMC_v_Minister_of_Public_Works_2023.md` |

**Key Principles:**
- Material errors in contract documents (BOQ, drawings) entitle contractor to variations
- BOQs must be project-specific and accurate - generic templates are insufficient
- Undisclosed site conditions (asbestos, contamination) trigger mandatory variation entitlements
- Variations cannot be dismissed as "contractor negligence" when caused by defective documents
- Employers cannot terminate contracts without first addressing fundamental document defects via variations
- PA must verify document accuracy during tender/contract preparation phase
- Wrongful termination exposes Employers to substantial damages including loss of profit

### Payment Disputes (Clause 6.25)
*Cases on payment certificates, withholding, and set-off*

| Case Name | Year | Court/Forum | Key Issue | Outcome | File |
|-----------|------|-------------|-----------|---------|------|
| Joob Joob v Stocks Mavundla | 2009 | SCA | Legal status of payment certificates | Payment certificates are "binding and equivalent to cash" | `Joob_Joob_v_Stocks_Mavundla_2009.md` |
| Inyatsi v National DPW | 2024 | High Court | Final Payment Certificate enforceability | Department bound to pay certified amount; certificate is liquid debt | `Inyatsi_v_National_DPW_2024.md` |
| Group Five v IDT | 2025 | High Court | Payment despite outstanding defects | Payment must be made within 21 days; defects handled separately under Clause 27 | `Group_Five_v_IDT_2025.md` |
| Randcon v Florida Twin Estates | Unknown | High Court | Employer disputing certificate validity | Very limited grounds to dispute (fraud only); Employer's remedy is against PA/QS | `Randcon_v_Florida_Twin_Estates.md` |
| Synthesis v SBTJ | Unknown | High Court | Payment certificates after termination | Certificates remain enforceable post-termination; notice of dissatisfaction requires arbitration follow-through | `Synthesis_v_SBTJ.md` |

**Key Principles:**
- **FOUNDATIONAL:** Payment certificates are "binding and equivalent to cash" (Joob Joob SCA)
- Final Payment Certificates create liquid acknowledgments of debt
- Payment obligations are immediate (21 days under JBCC) and unconditional
- Defects do NOT suspend payment obligation - defects handled separately under Clause 27
- Principle of reciprocity does NOT apply - payment and defect rectification are separate processes
- Very limited grounds to dispute certificates: fraud, collusion, or manifest error only
- Employer's remedy for incorrect certificate is to sue PA/QS for damages, NOT withhold payment from contractor
- Payment certificates remain enforceable even after contract termination
- State entities and municipalities fully bound by payment certificates

### Practical/Final Completion (Clauses 6.27-6.28)
*Cases on completion certificates and defects liability*

| Case Name | Year | Court/Forum | Key Issue | Outcome | File |
|-----------|------|-------------|-----------|---------|------|
| Group Five v IDT | 2025 | High Court | FC Certificate with noted defects | FC Certificate can be issued with defects on snag list; payment not conditional on rectification | `Group_Five_v_IDT_2025.md` |

**Key Principles:**
- Practical and Final Completion Certificates can be issued even when defects exist
- Defects must be noted on snag list attached to certificates
- Defects Liability Period (DLP) is the mechanism for managing post-completion defects
- Payment is NOT conditional on rectification of defects noted on snag list
- Building must be "substantially complete" and usable for PC to be issued
- Minor defects do not prevent certification - major defects requiring significant rework may prevent PC
- Snag list is legally binding - contractor obliged to rectify items during DLP

### Principal Agent Duties & Liability
*Cases defining PA responsibilities, standard of care, and liability*

| Case Name | Year | Court/Forum | Key Issue | Outcome | File |
|-----------|------|-------------|-----------|---------|------|
| Randcon v Florida Twin Estates | Unknown | High Court | PA liability for incorrect certification | Employer's remedy for wrong certificate is to sue PA/QS for damages, not refuse payment | `Randcon_v_Florida_Twin_Estates.md` |
| Canton Trading v Fanti Bekker | 2022 | SCA | PA professional appointment; arbitration clause validity | PA service agreements must be signed to be enforceable; arbitration clause existence disputed | `Canton_Trading_v_Fanti_Bekker_2022.md` |
| Pro-Khaya v City of Cape Town | 2025 | HC Western Cape | PA's EOT determinations subject to adjudication | PA acts as expert when certifying; determinations immediately binding | `Pro-Khaya_v_City_of_Cape_Town_2025.md` |

**Key Principles:**
- PA creates binding legal obligations for Employer through certification and determinations
- PA can be sued by Employer for professional negligence if certification is incorrect
- Employer cannot refuse payment to contractor for PA errors - must sue PA separately
- PA service agreements should be formally signed and documented
- Unsigned PA appointment documents may not be enforceable
- PA acts as expert (not arbitrator) when making determinations under JBCC
- PA owes duty of care to Employer but must act impartially when certifying
- Professional Indemnity insurance essential for PA role
- Standard of care: reasonable skill expected of competent PM professional

### Adjudication & Dispute Resolution
*Cases on JBCC adjudication procedures and enforcement*

| Case Name | Year | Court/Forum | Key Issue | Outcome | File |
|-----------|------|-------------|-----------|---------|------|
| Stefanutti Stocks v S8 Property | 2013 | SCA | Foundation case on adjudication enforceability | "Pay now, argue later" - determinations immediately binding; pro-enforcement stance | `Stefanutti_Stocks_v_S8_Property_2013.md` |
| Pro-Khaya v City of Cape Town | 2025 | HC Western Cape | Adjudication enforcement; grounds for review | Very limited review grounds (fraud/collusion); 10-day notice deadline critical | `Pro-Khaya_v_City_of_Cape_Town_2025.md` |
| Zingwazi v EC Human Settlements | 2021 | High Court | Constitutionality of adjudication | Adjudication constitutional; state entities fully bound | `Zingwazi_v_EC_Human_Settlements_2021.md` |
| GVK-Siya Zama v Slim | 2025 | High Court | Delay tactics in adjudication | Courts impose punitive costs for obstruction; strict timeline enforcement | `GVK-Siya_Zama_v_Slim_2025.md` |
| Synthesis v SBTJ | Unknown | High Court | Notice of dissatisfaction follow-through | Notice alone insufficient - must pursue arbitration or determination becomes enforceable | `Synthesis_v_SBTJ.md` |
| Canton Trading v Fanti Bekker | 2022 | SCA | Arbitration agreement existence | Court (not arbitrator) determines if arbitration agreement exists; separability limits | `Canton_Trading_v_Fanti_Bekker_2022.md` |

**Key Principles:**
- **FOUNDATIONAL:** "Pay now, argue later" - adjudication determinations immediately binding (Stefanutti Stocks SCA 2013)
- Determinations provisionally binding unless overturned by subsequent arbitration/litigation
- Very limited grounds for court review: fraud, collusion, capriciousness, manifest injustice only
- **10-working-day notice of dissatisfaction deadline** - failure renders determination final and enforceable
- Notice of dissatisfaction alone is insufficient - must follow through with arbitration proceedings
- Courts take pro-enforcement stance - will not tolerate delay tactics or obstruction
- Punitive costs orders imposed for tactical delays or threatening adjudicators
- Adjudication timelines are strict and rigorously enforced
- Parallel arbitration/litigation does NOT suspend adjudication timelines
- Adjudication is constitutional and public policy-compliant
- State entities, municipalities, and private parties all bound equally
- Adjudicators act as experts with wide discretion, not arbitrators
- Arbitration Act does not apply to JBCC adjudication
- When arbitration agreement existence is disputed, courts decide (not arbitrator)
