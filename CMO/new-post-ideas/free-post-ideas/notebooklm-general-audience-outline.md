## Section 3: Foundation Setup

### Step 1: Curate Sources You Trust
*(Same methodology, different examples)*

**The process:**
1. What decisions or topics do you return to repeatedly?
2. What sources do you actually trust in this area?
3. Start with 5-10 core documents

**Where to find quality sources by domain:**

| Domain | Source Types | Where to Find |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| Career | Salary guides, job postings, company reviews | Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, LinkedIn, company 10-Ks |
| Health | Medical guidelines, treatment summaries, insurance policies | Mayo Clinic, NIH, your actual insurance docs |
| Finance | Tax guides, investment research, policy documents | IRS publications, Bogleheads wiki, your statements |
| Learning | Textbooks, course materials, reference guides | Your purchased courses, library PDFs, official docs |
| Purchases | Expert reviews, spec sheets, comparison guides | Wirecutter, rtings.com, manufacturer specs |

**Common mistakes:**
- Uploading random blog posts (garbage in = garbage out)
- Using outdated information (check dates!)
- Skipping your own notes (your experience is valuable data)

### Step 2: Organize by Life Domain
*(Same principle, different categories)*

Suggested starter notebooks:
- **Career & Work** - salary data, job descriptions, performance reviews, skill requirements
- **Health & Wellness** - medical records, treatment options, insurance policies, research
- **Money & Finance** - tax documents, investment guides, major purchase research
- **Learning & Growth** - course materials, books, skill development resources

**Keep notebooks focused:** Career questions shouldn't surface health data

### Step 3: Set Custom Personas

**Templates for different domains:**

```
Career Notebook:
You are a career strategist and negotiation coach. Be direct and analytical.
Focus on concrete data points and actionable recommendations. When discussing
salary, always reference market data from the uploaded sources.
```

```
Health Notebook:
You are a health research assistant. Be thorough and balanced. Always
distinguish between what the sources say vs. what requires professional
medical advice. Cite specific sources for any claims.
```

```
Learning Notebook:
You are a learning coach and subject matter tutor. Explain concepts clearly.
When I'm confused, break things down further. Quiz me to test understanding.
Connect new concepts to what I already know.
```

```
Purchase Research Notebook:
You are a consumer research analyst. Be objective about tradeoffs. Summarize
expert opinions, highlight consensus and disagreements. Help me understand
what matters most for MY use case.
```

### Step 4: Build Learning Loops
*(Same methodology—this is universal)*

**Before any significant decision:**
1. Open relevant notebook
2. Have focused conversation about your specific situation
3. Generate briefing doc with key points
4. Use mind map to see connections

**After any significant outcome:**
1. Document what happened (what worked, what didn't)
2. Upload notes to relevant notebook
3. Your system compounds over time

**The compound effect example:**

- Month 1: Ask about salary negotiation → Get textbook advice
- Month 3: After uploading your actual negotiation notes → Recommendations get sharper
- Month 6: Ask about next negotiation → System references YOUR patterns: "In your previous role change, you succeeded when you led with the competing offer rather than market data"

## Section 4: The Three Personal Knowledge Systems

### System 1: The Decision Preparation System
**Purpose:** Walk into important conversations armed with facts

**Use cases:**
- Salary/raise negotiations
- Vendor/contractor negotiations
- Major purchase decisions
- Lease negotiations
- Medical treatment decisions

**Notebook contents:**
- Market data relevant to your decision
- Your own research and notes
- Comparison documents
- Expert opinions/reviews
- Your past experience notes

**The workflow:**

```
PROMPT: DECISION CONTEXT SETUP

I'm preparing for [DECISION TYPE] regarding [SPECIFIC SITUATION].

Key factors I need to consider:
1. [FACTOR 1]
2. [FACTOR 2]
3. [FACTOR 3]

My ideal outcome: [WHAT YOU WANT]

Based on the sources in this notebook:
- What's my strongest position?
- What data supports my case?
- What counter-arguments should I anticipate?
- What questions should I ask?
```

**Real-world example:**

*Situation:* Negotiating salary for a new role

*20-minute NotebookLM session delivered:*
- Salary range from 3 different data sources (with citations)
- Company's recent funding and growth trajectory
- Specific phrases that worked in documented negotiations
- Counter-arguments for "we don't have budget" objection
- Questions to ask about equity, bonus structure, review timeline

*Result:* Negotiated $12K above initial offer using specific market data

### System 2: The Research Synthesis System
**Purpose:** Turn scattered information into clear understanding

**Use cases:**
- Health decisions (treatment options, second opinions)
- Major purchases (cars, homes, appliances)
- Life decisions (where to live, school choices)
- Understanding complex topics (taxes, insurance, legal matters)

**Notebook contents:**
- Expert sources on the topic
- Official documentation
- Your specific situation details
- Questions you need answered

**The workflow:**

```
PROMPT: RESEARCH SYNTHESIS

I'm researching [TOPIC] because [WHY IT MATTERS TO YOU].

My specific situation: [RELEVANT DETAILS]

Based on all sources in this notebook:
1. What are my main options?
2. What are the pros/cons of each for MY situation?
3. Where do experts agree vs. disagree?
4. What questions should I ask [RELEVANT PROFESSIONAL]?
5. What would you recommend I prioritize learning more about?
```

**Real-world example:**

*Situation:* Deciding between treatment options after diagnosis

*30-minute NotebookLM session delivered:*
- Summary of each treatment from medical sources
- Success rates and side effects (with citations)
- Questions to ask the specialist
- Factors specific to patient's age/situation
- What insurance typically covers

*Result:* Walked into appointment with informed questions, had productive conversation with doctor, felt confident in final decision

### System 3: The Learning Acceleration System
**Purpose:** Master new subjects faster by building a personal knowledge base

**Use cases:**
- Professional skill development
- Certification preparation
- Hobby mastery
- Learning for a new role/industry
- Academic study

**Notebook contents:**
- Course materials and textbooks
- Your notes and highlights
- Practice problems and solutions
- Expert content in the field
- Your questions and confusion points

**The workflow:**

```
PROMPT: LEARNING SESSION

I'm studying [TOPIC/SKILL] with the goal of [SPECIFIC OUTCOME].

What I think I understand: [YOUR CURRENT UNDERSTANDING]

What I'm confused about: [SPECIFIC CONFUSION]

Based on the learning materials in this notebook:
1. Is my understanding correct? Where am I off?
2. Explain [CONFUSING CONCEPT] in simpler terms
3. How does this connect to [RELATED CONCEPT]?
4. Give me a practice question to test my understanding
5. What should I focus on next?
```

**Real-world example:**

*Situation:* Preparing for AWS certification exam

*Learning system results over 6 weeks:*
- Uploaded course materials, practice exams, AWS documentation
- Used NotebookLM as tutor for confusing concepts
- Generated custom practice questions from weak areas
- Created audio summaries for commute listening
- Documented which explanations finally "clicked"

*Result:* Passed certification on first attempt, retained knowledge better than previous self-study attempts

### When NOT to Use These Systems

**Honesty section (important for trust):**

- **Live follow-up questions:** When conversation goes off-script, NotebookLM can't help in the moment
- **Rapidly changing information:** If your sources are outdated, answers will be too
- **Highly personal advice:** NotebookLM synthesizes information, it doesn't know YOU
- **Professional expertise replacement:** Use for research, not as substitute for doctors/lawyers/accountants
- **When you haven't verified sources:** The system is only as good as what you upload

## Section 6: Common Pitfalls and Solutions

*(Keep this section mostly as-is—universally applicable)*

1. **Poor source quality** → Garbage in, garbage out
2. **Over-reliance on AI** → Always apply human judgment
3. **Set and forget** → Systems need maintenance
4. **Too many notebooks** → Start with 2-3, expand only when needed
5. **Skipping verification** → High-stakes = verify everything

### The Three Non-Negotiables
1. **Source quality** - Only upload sources you'd cite in important conversations
2. **Regular verification** - Click through to sources for important decisions
3. **Continuous learning** - Document outcomes, upload notes, let system compound
